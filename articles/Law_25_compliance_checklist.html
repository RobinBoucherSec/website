Quebec’s Law 25 Compliance Checklist: A Stress-Free Guide for Businesses

By Robin Boucher | 2025-05-27

Reading Time: 6 minutes

Introduction (Hook + Value Promise)

Quebec’s Law 25 (formerly Bill 64) is one of North America’s strictest data privacy laws, with fines up to $25 million or 4% of global revenue. But compliance doesn’t have to be overwhelming.

This checklist breaks down exactly what businesses need to do, with:

  • Clear explanations (no legal jargon)
  • Key deadlines you can’t miss
  • Free tools and resources to save time and money

Tip: A Montreal bakery was fined $140,000 for failing to encrypt customer data. Don’t let this be your business.

Requirement Deadline Penalty Risk

Step-by-Step Guide / Checklist

1. Data Mapping (Do This First!)

Requirement: Know where personal data is stored (Article 3.2).

How to Comply:

  • List all systems holding customer/employee data (e.g., CRM, payroll).
  • Use this free tool: CAI Data Inventory Sheet.

Pro Tip: Cloud providers like AWS and Google Cloud now offer Quebec-based storage options to comply with Article 17.

2. Appoint a Privacy Officer

Requirement: Assign someone responsible for data protection (Article 3.1).

Who Qualifies?

  • An internal employee (e.g., IT manager) or an external consultant.
  • No formal certification required, but training is strongly advised.

Free Training Option:

  • AMF’s 1-Hour Privacy Course (Government-approved)

3. Encrypt Everything

Requirement: Protect data in transit and at rest (Article 8.1).

Recommended Tools:

Data Type Tool Cost
Emails ProtonMail Free / $4.99/month
Files VeraCrypt Free
Cloud Storage Tresorit $10.42/month

Warning: SMS and standard email are not compliant for sensitive data.

4. Mandatory Employee Training

Requirement: Annual privacy training (Article 6.1).

What to Cover:

  • How to identify phishing emails
  • Secure password practices
  • Internal breach reporting procedures

Free Resources:

  • C3P Phishing Simulation Kit (Government of Canada)
  • Law 25 Explainer Video (Quebec-made)

5. Breach Response Plan

Requirement: Report breaches to CAI within 72 hours (Article 3.5).

Three-Step Response Process:

  1. Contain: Disconnect affected systems.
  2. Assess: Identify whose data was exposed.
  3. Report: Submit notification via the CAI Online Portal.

Real Example: A Quebec HVAC company avoided penalties by reporting a breach in 68 hours and offering free credit monitoring.

Budget-Friendly Compliance Tips

  • Under 50 employees? Use free tools like Bitwarden (password manager) and ProtonMail.
  • DIY Training: Host a lunch-and-learn using CAI’s presentation slides.
  • Affordable Encryption: VeraCrypt + Tresorit costs under $15/month.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Believing small businesses are exempt — Law 25 applies to all companies handling Quebecers’ data.
  • Using unencrypted tools like Google Forms — switch to CryptPad for surveys.
  • Ignoring vendor contracts — third-party compliance is mandatory under Article 18.3.

Final Thought

Law 25 is not about fear—it’s about trust. Customers reward businesses that protect their data. Start with one item from this checklist today, and you’ll be far ahead of most competitors.
